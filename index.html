Swimming World!

There is evidence that people have been swimming for thousands of years. The Cave of Swimmers, located in Egypt's Western Desert, has rock paintings of people swimming. These rock paintings are considered difficult to date, but some estimates date them between 6,000-9,000 years old.

In Greek and Roman civilizations, swimming was a portion of martial arts training and early education for boys. There is evidence that the Romans also built swimming pools, including the first heated swimming pool built in the first century BCE. Additionally, ancient Greeks held swimming races. There is also evidence that swimming races occurred in Asian countries as early as the first century BCE. The first book about swimming was written in 1538 titled Colymbetes by Nicolas Wynman.

Learn to swim

Swimming is generally better for the body than land exercises, as the natural ability to float in water helps avoid the inevitable shocks that can lead to injury. In the water, the weight of a person is approximately 10% of their normal weight, and the range of mobility is much greater, especially for people who need to get fit the most and have limited mobility since the water supports the weight of the members.

Therefore, it is a good choice for those who want to exercise, and may have problems with the practice of exercises on land. For example, swimming can be ideal for pregnant women, for those who suffer from arthritis, or for those who suffer from back and weight problems.
